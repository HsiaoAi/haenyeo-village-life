STONE
Dol Hareubang
Dol Hareubang · Stone Grandfather
Carved from porous Jeju basalt, these stone grandfathers once stood at village and fortress gates as guardians — warding off evil spirits and watching over all who passed.
Their mushroom hat, wide eyes and long nose are unmistakable. Folklore says rubbing the nose brings a son; the ears, a daughter. This little one cradles a yuchae blossom — Jeju's golden spring canola.
FIRE
Bulteok
Bulteok · The divers' fire
A low ring of basalt stone on the shore, open to the sky. Around its fire the haenyeo undress, warm their bodies before the cold dive, and thaw out again when they surface.
More than a hearth, the bulteok is the village's living room — where the elders pass down the diving songs, read the tides, and decide together who dives where. The sanggun, the master diver, keeps the warmest seat.
